Google Search Landscape (Oct 2024 - Oct 2025)

Analyzing search behavior reveals user intent and seasonality. "Furniture" remains the dominant, high-volume search term. However, "Kitchen Design" and "Doors" show consistent, specific interest, while "Office Furniture" is a smaller, niche query. A clear seasonal dip is visible around March-April, with a significant peak in October-November.

Search Term Interest Over Time (Pakistan)

Key Takeaway: While "Furniture" provides volume, targeting the "Kitchen Design" and "Doors" keywords during their stable periods can capture high-intent customers. The Oct-Nov peak is the critical sales period to maximize ad spend.

Regional Interest in Growth Categories

Search intensity for "Kitchen Design" and "Doors" is highest in developing regions, suggesting new construction and renovation projects are key drivers. Punjab and Sindh, while large markets, show more saturated search interest relative to the "Furniture" baseline.

Paid Advertising Performance & Forecast

The Google Ads forecast indicates a critical discrepancy between devices. While Mobile devices drive the vast majority of impressions and clicks, Desktop users are significantly more engaged, boasting a Click-Through Rate (CTR) nearly double that of mobile. This suggests mobile landing pages may not be as optimized, or desktop users are more high-intent for B2B/high-value purchases.

Est. Clicks vs. Cost by Device

Mobile accounts for 90% of clicks and 82% of the cost. However, the cost per click (CPC) is much higher on Desktop ($0.35) than on Mobile ($0.15), indicating more competition for desktop users.

Est. Impressions Share by Device

The campaign's reach is overwhelmingly mobile, with 95% of all ad impressions served on mobile devices. This makes mobile ad creative and landing page experience the single most important factor for top-of-funnel performance.

The Desktop vs. Mobile CTR Gap

The most significant finding is the engagement gap. Desktop users are far more likely to click an ad, suggesting they are a more qualified, purchase-ready audience, likely for high-value items like kitchens or office furniture.

7.4% Desktop CTR
3.8% Mobile CTR

Key Takeaway: A dual strategy is required. Optimize mobile landing pages for speed and clarity to improve the 3.8% CTR, while creating dedicated, high-conversion funnels for the 7.4% CTR desktop audience.

SWOT Analysis

This analysis consolidates Interwood's position, highlighting the core tension between its established brand strength and the threat of new, digitally-native competitors.

Strengths

  • Premium brand recognition and trust.
  • Diverse product range (Home, Office, Kitchens).
  • Strong brand search volume ("interwood").
  • Established physical retail network.
  • Sophisticated tech stack (Hubspot, Klaviyo).

Weaknesses

  • Lower mobile CTR (3.8%) vs. Desktop (7.4%).
  • Potential for slow adaptation vs. agile startups.
  • Higher price point may be a barrier for online-first customers.
  • Website rank (#783,043) is solid but has room for SEO growth.

Opportunities

  • Optimize mobile landing pages to boost CVR.
  • Target "Kitchen Design" & "Doors" in growth regions (KP, Balochistan).
  • Create content hubs to capture non-branded SEO traffic.
  • Launch aggressive campaigns targeting competitor keywords.
  • Leverage Hubspot/Klaviyo for advanced retargeting.

Threats

  • Rapid rise of digital-first brands ("Life Standard").
  • Competitors capturing generic search terms.
  • Economic pressure favoring lower-cost alternatives.
  • Potential over-reliance on brand vs. generic SEO.

Digital Growth Strategy (A Trends Hive Proposal)

To capitalize on these findings, we propose a 3-phase strategy focusing on optimization, expansion, and market domination. This plan leverages existing strengths while directly addressing the competitive threats and optimization gaps.

Phase 1: OPTIMIZE

  • A/B Test Mobile Landing Pages: Rework pages for B2C categories (Home Furniture) to improve the 3.8% CTR.
  • Enhance Desktop Funnels: Create dedicated, high-intent landing pages for B2B (Office) and high-value B2C (Kitchens) to capitalize on the 7.4% CTR.
  • Refine Ad Copy: Ensure ad copy matches device intent (e.g., "Shop Now" for mobile, "Get Quote" for desktop).

Phase 2: EXPAND

  • Regional SEO/Ad Campaigns: Launch targeted campaigns for "Kitchen Design" and "Doors" in KP, Balochistan, and Gilgit-Baltistan.
  • Content Hub Development: Build SEO-focused content around "modern kitchen design ideas," "office furniture layouts," etc., to capture non-branded search.
  • Competitor Ad Groups: Bid directly on rising competitor terms ("Life Standard Furniture") to capture their audience.

Phase 3: DOMINATE

  • Full-Funnel Retargeting: Use Klaviyo/Hubspot data to serve dynamic ads based on user behavior (e.g., retarget "Kitchen" browsers with "Door" ads).
  • Programmatic Display: Expand reach beyond search to programmatic display ads, targeting users based on demographic and behavioral data.
  • Brand-Defense Campaigns: Solidify dominance by bidding aggressively on all brand + category combinations.
